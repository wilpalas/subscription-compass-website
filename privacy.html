1. Introduction

Subscription Compass ("we," "our," or "the Extension") is a browser extension designed to help users locate subscription cancellation buttons on websites. We are committed to protecting your privacy and being transparent about our data practices.

This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect (spoiler: almost nothing), how we use it, and your rights regarding your data.

3. Information We DO Collect (Locally Only)

The Extension stores minimal data locally on your device only. This data never leaves your browser:

3.1 Success/Failure Logs (Optional)

  • What: Records of whether the Extension found a cancel button on specific sites
  • Stored: Locally in Chrome's storage (never transmitted)
  • Purpose: To improve the Extension's accuracy
  • Contains: Site name, timestamp, success/failure status
  • Control: You can clear this data anytime in the Extension settings

3.2 Extension Settings

  • What: Your preferences (e.g., accessibility settings, banner preferences)
  • Stored: Locally in Chrome's storage
  • Purpose: To remember your preferences
Important: All data is stored using Chrome's Local Storage API and remains on your device. We have no servers, no databases, and no way to access your data.

4. How the Extension Works

Understanding how we work helps explain our privacy approach:

  1. Page Scanning: When you visit a supported website, the Extension scans the page's HTML/DOM for cancel buttons
  2. Button Detection: We use CSS selectors and AI analysis to identify cancellation buttons
  3. Visual Guidance: If found, we display a banner and highlight the button
  4. User Control: You click the button yourself‚Äîwe never interact with your accounts

Key Point: We only read the page structure (HTML/DOM). We never read form inputs, passwords, or personal data fields.

5. Permissions Explained

Chrome requires us to declare permissions. Here's what each one means:

5.1 "activeTab" Permission

  • What it allows: Access to the current tab when you interact with the Extension
  • Why we need it: To scan the page for cancel buttons
  • What we do: Read page structure (HTML/DOM) to find buttons
  • What we DON'T do: Access other tabs, read form data, or track browsing

5.2 "storage" Permission

  • What it allows: Store data locally in your browser
  • Why we need it: Save your preferences and success/failure logs
  • What we do: Store settings and logs locally only
  • What we DON'T do: Sync data to cloud or transmit to servers

5.3 "scripting" Permission

  • What it allows: Run scripts on web pages
  • Why we need it: Inject our button detection and banner display code
  • What we do: Add visual overlays and highlight buttons
  • What we DON'T do: Modify page content, submit forms, or interact with accounts

5.4 "alarms" Permission

  • What it allows: Schedule periodic tasks
  • Why we need it: Check for selector updates (future feature)
  • What we do: Potentially update button selectors periodically
  • What we DON'T do: Track usage or send data
